# Texas TEKS — 4th Grade Science Standards (Adopted 2021)

## Overview
Grade 4 science covers five strands. Content builds on Grade 3 and prepares for Grade 5.
STAAR Science is tested in Grade 5, but Grade 4 content appears on that test.

---

## Strand 1: Scientific and Engineering Practices (4.1–4.3)

### 4.1 — Scientific Inquiry
- Ask questions, plan and conduct investigations
- Methods: descriptive, correlative, comparative, experimental
- Use tools: thermometers, meter sticks, balances, graduated cylinders, beakers, spring scales, hot plates, magnets, collecting nets, microscopes

### 4.2 — Data Analysis
- Collect, record, and analyze data using tables, bar graphs, dot plots
- Identify features, patterns, relationships in data
- Models have limitations but help understand ideas

### 4.3 — Evidence-Based Explanations
- Develop explanations supported by evidence
- Communicate findings and conclusions
- Propose solutions to problems

**Question generation notes:**
- Include questions that ask students to read data from tables/graphs
- Ask "what would happen if..." to test prediction skills
- Include experiment design questions ("How would you test...")

---

## Strand 2: Matter and Its Properties (4.6)

### 4.6A — Physical Properties
- Mass, magnetism, relative density, solubility in water
- Ability to conduct or insulate thermal or electric energy
- Students should be able to classify objects by these properties

### 4.6B — Physical vs Chemical Changes
- Classify changes as physical or chemical
- Physical: cutting, folding, dissolving, freezing/melting, mixing
- Chemical: burning, rusting, cooking, combining baking soda + vinegar

### 4.6C — Mixtures
- Identify mixtures and ways to separate them
- Filtering, evaporation, magnetism, screening

**Example question contexts:**
- Kitchen chemistry (cooking = chemical change, cutting vegetables = physical)
- Building materials (conductors vs insulators)
- Cleaning up a mess (separating mixtures)

---

## Strand 3: Force, Motion, and Energy (4.7–4.8)

### 4.7 — Forces
- Plan and conduct investigations on forces
- Gravity, friction, magnetism
- Contact forces vs forces at a distance
- Patterns of force interactions

### 4.8A — Energy Forms
- Light, heat/thermal, sound, electrical, mechanical energy
- Design experiments to test energy interactions

### 4.8B — Energy Transfer
- Demonstrate energy transfers
- Examples: heat transfer (conduction), light reflection, sound vibration
- Electrical circuits (open and closed)

**Example question contexts:**
- Sports (friction on different surfaces, gravity on a ball)
- Music (sound energy, vibrations)
- Home electricity (circuits, conductors, switches)
- Cooking (heat transfer)

---

## Strand 4: Earth and Space (4.9–4.11)

### 4.9 — Sun, Earth, Moon
- Rotation causes day/night
- Revolution causes seasons
- Moon phases (pattern recognition)
- Positions of Sun, Earth, Moon

### 4.10A — Weather
- Measure and record weather data
- Temperature, wind speed/direction, precipitation
- Weather instruments

### 4.10B — Earth's Surface Changes
- Weathering, erosion, deposition
- Effects of wind, water, ice
- Slow vs rapid changes (earthquake, volcano, flood vs weathering)

### 4.11A — Natural Resources
- Renewable vs nonrenewable
- Examples: water, soil, trees (renewable) vs coal, oil, natural gas (nonrenewable)
- Conservation and responsible use

### 4.11B — Fossils
- What fossils tell us about past environments
- Types of fossils
- How fossils form

**Example question contexts:**
- Weather reports and forecasting
- Moon observation journals
- Local geography and erosion
- Energy sources and the environment

---

## Strand 5: Organisms and Environments (4.12–4.13)

### 4.12A — Ecosystems
- Producers, consumers, decomposers
- Food chains and food webs
- Energy flow through ecosystems
- Roles in an ecosystem

### 4.12B — Life Cycles
- Complete vs incomplete metamorphosis
- Plant life cycles
- Compare life cycles across organisms

### 4.13A — Adaptations
- Structures and behaviors that help survival
- How adaptations relate to environment
- Examples: camouflage, migration, hibernation, thorns, deep roots

### 4.13B — Environmental Changes
- How changes affect organisms
- Natural changes (drought, fire, flood)
- Human-caused changes (pollution, habitat loss, conservation)

**Example question contexts:**
- Local wildlife and habitats
- Garden ecosystems
- Animal adaptations (connect to Wolfkid universe — wolf pack behavior!)
- Environmental news and conservation

## STAAR Readiness Notes
- Grade 5 STAAR tests both Grade 4 and Grade 5 content
- Focus on application, not memorization
- Questions often include data tables, diagrams, or scenarios
- "Best supports" and "which conclusion" are common stems
- CER structure directly maps to STAAR constructed response format
