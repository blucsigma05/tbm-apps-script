# Pre-K Learning Milestones — JJ (Age 4, Entering Preschool September 2026)

## Overview
JJ is 4 years old, preparing for preschool entry in September 2026.
Focus areas: letter recognition, name writing, number sense, fine motor development,
social-emotional skills, and early science concepts.

## Pre-K Readiness Goals (by September 2026)

### Priority 1: Name Writing
- [ ] Recognize all letters in her name (J)
- [ ] Write first letter of name independently
- [ ] Trace full name with assistance
- [ ] Write name independently (stretch goal)

### Priority 2: Letter Recognition
**Phase 1 (Mar-Apr):** Letters in her name + A, B, C, M, S
**Phase 2 (May-Jun):** D, E, F, G, H, K, L
**Phase 3 (Jul-Aug):** N, O, P, R, T, W
**Phase 4 (Sep):** Remaining letters, review all

For each letter, progression is:
1. Visual recognition (can point to it)
2. Name it when shown
3. Trace it with finger
4. Associate beginning sound
5. Write it independently

### Priority 3: Number Sense
- Count objects 1-10 (one-to-one correspondence)
- Recognize written numerals 1-10
- Count by rote to 20
- Understand "more" and "less"
- Simple patterns (AB, ABC)

### Priority 4: Colors and Shapes
- Name primary colors (red, blue, yellow)
- Name secondary colors (green, orange, purple)
- Identify basic shapes: circle, square, triangle, rectangle
- Match shapes to real-world objects

### Priority 5: Fine Motor
- Hold crayon/pencil with proper grip (modified tripod)
- Draw circles, lines, crosses
- Use scissors to cut straight lines
- String beads, use playdough

### Priority 6: Social-Emotional
- Take turns
- Express feelings with words
- Follow 2-3 step directions
- Play cooperatively with peers

## Activity Types (Rotate Daily)

### Letter Activities
- **Trace & Sparkle**: Finger trace letter on screen with sparkle trail
- **Letter Hunt**: Find the target letter among 3-4 options
- **Sound Match**: "What starts with B? Ball, Cat, or Dog?"
- **Name Builder**: Arrange letter tiles to spell name
- **Letter Art**: Decorate a big letter with colors/stickers

### Number Activities
- **Count & Tap**: Tap objects to count them (1-10)
- **Number Match**: Match numeral to quantity
- **More or Less**: Compare two groups
- **Pattern Builder**: Complete simple patterns
- **Number Trace**: Trace numerals with finger

### Science Activities (Simple Concepts)
- **Weather Watch**: What's the weather today? (sunny/rainy/cloudy/windy)
- **Plant Growth**: Water makes plants grow, sun gives energy
- **Animal Homes**: Where do different animals live?
- **5 Senses**: What do you see/hear/feel/smell/taste?
- **Sink or Float**: Predict and test

### Creative/Social Activities
- **Feelings Check-In**: How do you feel today? (happy/sad/mad/excited)
- **Story Time**: Listen and answer simple questions
- **Color Mixing**: What happens when you mix red + yellow?
- **Sort & Group**: Group objects by color, shape, or size

---

## Session Design Rules
- Maximum 10 minutes per activity session
- Always start with something she already knows (confidence builder)
- End with a celebration (stars, sparkles, positive reinforcement)
- Never more than 2-3 choices on screen at once
- Use audio prompts alongside visual
- Touch targets minimum 60px x 60px on tablet
- Allow "wrong" answers without penalty — just gentle redirect
- Include "your choice" moments (pick which letter, pick which color)
